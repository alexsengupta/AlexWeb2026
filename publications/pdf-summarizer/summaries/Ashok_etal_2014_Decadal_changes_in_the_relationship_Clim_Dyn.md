Monsoons are critical for agriculture and water supply in many regions. The Indian summer monsoon (ISMR), occurring between June and September, brings vital rainfall to India, while the Australian summer monsoon (ASMR) takes place from December to February. This paper investigates how ISMR influences ASMR, particularly the fluctuations in their relationship over time. 

The study is motivated by earlier observations that the link between these monsoons can weaken or strengthen depending on climatic conditions. The researchers utilized a combination of observed rainfall and sea surface temperature (SST) data spanning from 1900 to 2009 to analyze the correlation between ISMR and ASMR.
