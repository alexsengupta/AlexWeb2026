By leveraging EOFs, the new ZW3 index allows for a better representation of the variability in ZW3 patterns, regardless of their spatial configuration.

The researchers highlighted the ability of the new ZW3 index to capture a higher proportion of variance—approximately 40%—compared to earlier indices that only captured around 16%. This was achieved by utilizing both the magnitude and phase of the ZW3 pattern, which are essential for understanding the complex interactions within the atmosphere, such as the transport of energy and the impacts on Antarctic sea ice.
