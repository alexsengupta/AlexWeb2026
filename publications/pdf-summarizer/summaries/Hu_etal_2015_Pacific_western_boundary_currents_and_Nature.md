### Background/Context

Western boundary currents are swift, narrow oceanic currents located at the edges of oceanic gyres. The Pacific Ocean features several significant WBCs, including the Kuroshio Current and the East Australian Current in the subtropical gyres, along with the Mindanao Current and the New Guinea Coastal Undercurrent in tropical regions. Their dynamics are crucial to climate systems, especially as they are associated with the transfer of heat and moisture into the atmosphere, significantly affecting global weather patterns.
