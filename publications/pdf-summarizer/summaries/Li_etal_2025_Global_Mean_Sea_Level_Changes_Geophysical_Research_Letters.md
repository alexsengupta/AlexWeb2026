### Main Research/Argument

The researchers aimed to investigate how El Niño and La Niña events, which are part of the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO), influence global sea levels through changes in precipitation and land water storage. ENSO events are characterized by periodic warming (El Niño) or cooling (La Niña) of sea surface temperatures in the central and eastern Pacific Ocean, leading to widespread changes in weather patterns.

To explore this relationship, the team analyzed satellite data on sea level heights from 1993 to 2022, alongside data on precipitation and land water storage. They used various datasets, including global monthly precipitation data and satellite gravity measurements, to assess changes in land water mass during significant ENSO events. The research methodology involved calculating precipitation anomalies and examining the effects on sea level, focusing on five La Niña events and seven El Niño events.
