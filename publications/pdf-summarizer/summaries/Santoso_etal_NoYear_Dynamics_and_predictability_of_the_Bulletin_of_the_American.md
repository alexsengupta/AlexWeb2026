Background/Context:  
The El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) is a significant climate phenomenon that influences weather patterns worldwide, particularly in Australia. It consists of two opposing phases: El Niño, which brings warm ocean temperatures and often leads to droughts, and La Niña, which results in cooler ocean temperatures and typically causes increased rainfall and flooding. These variations have critical implications for agriculture, water supply, and disaster management in Australia, a country frequently affected by the extremes of ENSO. For instance, the 2015/16 El Niño was notably intense, yet its prediction posed challenges, demonstrating the complex dynamics of these climate events. The need for improved understanding and prediction of ENSO dynamics has been underscored by the dramatic impacts of ENSO on Australian climate, prompting a national workshop in 2017 to address these issues.

They emphasized that the characteristics of ENSO events can vary significantly, complicating predictions. The authors also discussed the importance of various factors, such as ocean temperatures, atmospheric patterns, and local climate interactions, in shaping ENSO events and their impacts.
