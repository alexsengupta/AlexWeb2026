The core of their research focused on "isotherms," which are lines that connect points of equal temperature in the ocean. By examining how these temperature boundaries shift over time, they aimed to understand the dynamics of marine species’ migrations. 

For example, think of isotherms as the borders between different temperature zones in the ocean. This means that marine species may also experience sudden changes in their habitats, making it difficult for them to adapt quickly.

For instance, the researchers highlighted that many economically important fish species are already showing signs of migration, which could lead to a "tropicalization" of fish stocks—where warmer-water species begin to dominate cooler-water fisheries.
