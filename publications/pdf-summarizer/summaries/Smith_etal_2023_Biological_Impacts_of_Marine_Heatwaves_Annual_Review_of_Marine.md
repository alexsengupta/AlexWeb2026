Background/Context  
Marine heatwaves (MHWs) are becoming more frequent and intense due to climate change, leading to severe impacts on marine ecosystems. The concern is not just academic; real-world examples, such as the unprecedented marine heatwave off the coast of Western Australia in 2011, have resulted in dramatic ecological shifts, including the die-off of kelp forests and widespread coral bleaching. The growing prevalence of MHWs poses risks not only to marine life but also to human societies that depend on healthy oceans for food, tourism, and other ecosystem services.
