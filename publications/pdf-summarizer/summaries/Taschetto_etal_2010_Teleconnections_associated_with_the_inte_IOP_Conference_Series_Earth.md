**Background/Context**

Climate scientists have long been fascinated by the El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO), a natural climate pattern that influences weather worldwide. Traditional El Niño events are characterized by warm sea surface temperatures (SST) in the eastern Pacific, which often lead to dry conditions in Australia. However, since the late 1970s, a new variant called El Niño Modoki has emerged, characterized by SST warming in the central-western Pacific. This study addresses how El Niño Modoki affects rainfall patterns in Australia, particularly during the monsoon season—an essential period for agriculture and water supply in the region.

In recent years, Australia has experienced increasingly unpredictable weather patterns, leading to severe droughts and floods. Understanding the mechanisms behind these shifts is crucial for improving weather predictions and managing water resources, especially as climate change continues to alter traditional weather patterns.
