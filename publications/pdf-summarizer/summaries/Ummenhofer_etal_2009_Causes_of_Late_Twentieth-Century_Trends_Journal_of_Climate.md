To explore the causes of precipitation trends in New Zealand, the researchers employed observational data and reanalysis datasets covering nearly three decades. The researchers specifically quantified the contributions of SAM and ENSO to changes in precipitation. For instance, the study found that the SAM contributed up to 80% to the decrease in precipitation over the North Island and significant percentages in the South Island as well.

The findings of the study are striking. Over the 27-year period analyzed, much of New Zealand experienced a decline in precipitation, particularly during the summer months. The researchers highlighted that the SAM's positive phase was linked to decreased rainfall due to changes in wind patterns that reduced the frequency of rain-bearing weather systems.
