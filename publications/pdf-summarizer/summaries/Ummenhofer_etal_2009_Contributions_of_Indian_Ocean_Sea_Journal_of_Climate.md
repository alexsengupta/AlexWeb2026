The researchers set out to determine how variations in Indian Ocean SST affect rainfall in East Africa, focusing on the phenomenon known as the Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD). They employed an atmospheric general circulation model (AGCM) to simulate and analyze the influence of these SST anomalies on rainfall patterns. Their approach involved creating ensemble simulations that included different SST perturbations, allowing them to isolate the effects of specific temperature anomalies in the ocean on atmospheric circulation and precipitation.

Interestingly, while the eastern cold pole of the IOD plays a role, its impact on enhancing rainfall is significantly less than that of the western warm pole.
