### Background/Context

The Indian summer monsoon is vital for the subcontinent, providing the majority of annual rainfall and sustaining agriculture. Historically, failures of the monsoon have led to severe droughts and famines, highlighting the importance of understanding the factors that influence monsoon variability. The ENSO phenomenon, particularly El Niño events, has been linked to disruptions in the Indian monsoon, typically resulting in reduced rainfall. However, this relationship is complex and can change over time. For instance, while El Niño events have often corresponded with monsoon failures, recent decades have shown a weakening of this connection. The research aims to shed light on these dynamics by examining the interplay between Pacific and Indian Ocean conditions.

The researchers conducted a comprehensive analysis of data spanning from 1877 to 2006, focusing on how Indian Ocean variability impacts the ENSO-monsoon relationship. They utilized a classification system for different types of ENSO and Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD) events to understand how these interactions have evolved over time. The approach involved analyzing historical rainfall data, sea surface temperatures, and atmospheric circulation patterns to illustrate how variations in the Indian Ocean can either amplify or diminish the effects of El Niño on the Indian monsoon.

2. **Role of Positive IOD Events:** When El Niño coincided with positive IOD events, the adverse effects on the monsoon were mitigated. In these cases, rainfall levels returned to normal, demonstrating that the Indian Ocean conditions could offset the drought-inducing effects of El Niño.

4. **Drought Severity Index:** The research employed the Palmer Drought Severity Index (PDSI) to assess drought conditions. It was found that more than 85% of El Niño events were associated with negative PDSI values, indicating severe drought conditions across much of India.
